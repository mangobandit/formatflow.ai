Why FormatFlow

Your files. Your rules. Your machine.

Most translation tools upload your document to their cloud and decide everything for you. FormatFlow does the opposite.

🔒

Local-first

FormatFlow Studio runs on your computer. Your documents are opened, checked and rebuilt locally — they never touch our servers. Translation goes straight from your machine to the AI provider, under your own API key, only when you run it. Built for NDA decks, board packs and legal documents.

🎛️

Parameters you control

You set the rules for every batch: target languages, model choice, tone preset and industry, your own client brief, glossary and translation memory, adapt-text-to-fit on or off, and a cap on estimated API cost. Nothing is decided for you.

👥

Per-client profiles Coming soon

Save those settings as a named profile per client or brand and reuse them in one click — your house rules, applied to every job with no reconfiguring. Today, glossaries and translation memory already carry each client's terminology across projects.

📋

Review-first overflow

Translated text often runs longer than the original. When it won't fit its box, FormatFlow flags it for review — you check a short list in QA Review and approve. Auto-fit only runs if you switched it on. Nothing is changed silently.

Getting Started

From install to exported files in six steps.

A quick guide to running your first batch translation in FormatFlow Studio for Windows.

01

Install

Download the Windows installer and run it. Approve the unknown-publisher prompt until the build is code-signed.

02

Activate your license

Open the License tab, paste your license key and click Activate. A short trial unlocks everything so you can test first.

03

Add documents

Go to Documents and drag in your PPTX or DOCX files — or drop them straight onto the Dashboard.

04

Choose languages & controls

Select your target languages and turn on Layout Preservation, Overflow Detection and Adapt Text to Fit.

05

Translate the batch

Click Translate Batch. Run Estimate Cost first if you want a projected API spend before committing.

06

Review & export

Check the QA Review tab for overflow and glossary issues, then Export Report for review-ready files.
